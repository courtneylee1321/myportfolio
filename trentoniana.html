Digitize Artifacts 2018

Created GIFs of artifacts from a local museum in Trenton, NJ

Duration

3 months

Overview

This class project was a part of an initiative to raise more awareness on the Trentoniana, local history, and genealogy collection.

With technology on the rise, more awareness must be brought on these artifacts since they reflect history that dates back to the American Revolution in 1776. 

Objective

Use photos to digitize artifacts in the Trentoniana so they can be seen on a website or another application.

Problem Statement

The Trenton Public Library holds the Trentoniana, local history and genealogy collection that contains photographs, books, oral histories, scrapbooks, business records, maps, newspapers, and more.

The library’s mission statement is to preserve the history & culture of the past so residents can be educated, inspired, and empowered to embrace their roots. Digitizing the collection will allow more people to be able to learn about the New Jersey capital, bringing more awareness to its importance in history.

Roles and Responsibilities

3 teammates and I picked 5 artifacts and took photos of them with different views such as front and sides. We took frame-by-frame photos, turning the artifact little by little with each photo.

Next, we put the frame-by-frame photos together on a timeline in Photoshop and created a GIF. We thought that this would make the artifacts look more alive and appealing as opposed to a still image.

My Role: I took pictures of the artifacts using a camera and tripod and uploaded them on a drive for my team to access. I created 4 out of 9 total GIFs.

Our Process

  1. Take Photos with Tripod

I was in charge of taking the photos since I was using my camera for our project. 

One of my teammates made a backdrop out of white foam board to make it easier for us to take pictures and crop on Photoshop. 

While I took the pictures, another teammate would rotate the object little by little to create the frame-by- frame images we wanted.

  1. Upload Photos on Photoshop

Each of the pictures was on separate layers and the backgrounds were removed one by one. 

We used the magic wand tool and the quick selection tool to remove the background.


Gear artifact images on Photoshop

  1. Create frames using layers

After the background was removed from all the images, we opened up the timeline window and created a new frame for each layer.

We set the duration for each frame to 0.1 seconds. We would then export the file as GIF. 


Each frame-by-frame photo was on it’s own layer and then transferred to a timeline to create the GIF, making it look like it is rotating

  1. Final products

After repeating this process multiple times, the following GIFs were created:

Conclusion


These GIFs are being saved to be displayed on the museum’s website. They are currently putting together a kiosk to place in the museum so visitors can use it to learn more about Trenton’s history.

This project was more meaningful and fulfilling from previous projects I’ve worked on since our work wasn’t just about getting a good grade. We were find a way to foster more learning about Trenton’s history to take place.

Unfortunately, more work needs to be done to find information about where these artifacts came from and the years they originated. Historians at the Trentoniana are still searching their origins.
